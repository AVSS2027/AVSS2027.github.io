<section class="section visa-section">
  <p class="section-kicker">For conference travel planning</p>
  <h2>Visa and Entry Information</h2>
  <p class="lead">
    AVSS 2027 participants are responsible for checking the entry requirements
    that apply to their own passport or travel document before travelling to
    Macau SAR. The information below is provided as a general planning guide and
    should be verified against the official Macao SAR Government information
    before departure.
  </p>

  <div class="notice">
    Entry policies can change with short notice. Participants are strongly
    encouraged to review the official information at least four weeks before
    travel and again shortly before departure.
  </div>

  <div class="visa-card-grid">
    <article class="visa-card">
      <span>01</span>
      <h3>Visa-Exempt Entry</h3>
      <p>
        Macau SAR provides visa or entry-permit exemption to nationals of many
        countries and regions for short tourism or business visits. The
        permitted length of stay depends on the passport or travel document and
        may vary, for example, between 14 days, 30 days, 90 days, 180 days, or a
        longer period for specific document holders.
      </p>
      <p>
        Common examples in conference planning materials include many European
        countries, the United Kingdom, Ireland, Brazil, Israel, the United
        States, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, Korea, Singapore,
        Malaysia, and South Africa. Participants should always verify the exact
        rule for their own passport or travel document.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="visa-card">
      <span>02</span>
      <h3>Entry Permit on Arrival</h3>
      <p>
        For many travelers who are not covered by visa-exempt entry, an entry
        permit, also commonly described as visa upon arrival, may be available
        at Macau SAR immigration checkpoints, subject to official rules and
        immigration approval.
      </p>
      <p>
        This option is generally intended for short visits such as tourism,
        business travel, or conference attendance. Participants who need to stay
        longer, work, or undertake activities beyond ordinary conference
        participation should apply for the appropriate permission in advance.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="visa-card">
      <span>03</span>
      <h3>Visa Required Before Arrival</h3>
      <p>
        According to the official Macao SAR Government information, nationals of
        the following countries are required to obtain a Macao visa before
        arrival and cannot apply for an entry permit on arrival:
      </p>
      <ul class="visa-country-list">
        <li>Bangladesh</li>
        <li>Nepal</li>
        <li>Nigeria</li>
        <li>Pakistan</li>
        <li>Sri Lanka</li>
        <li>Vietnam</li>
      </ul>
      <p>
        Authors, speakers, and attendees from these countries should begin the
        visa process early and allow sufficient time before travelling.
      </p>
    </article>
  </div>

  <h3>Notes for Mainland China Residents</h3>
  <p>
    Travel from Mainland China to Macau SAR is normally not handled as a
    standard visa process. Mainland China residents usually need an Exit-Entry
    Permit for Travelling to and from Hong Kong and Macao and a valid
    endorsement, such as an individual travel or business endorsement.
  </p>

  <h3>Before You Travel</h3>
  <ul class="clean-list visa-checklist">
    <li>Check the rule that applies to your passport or travel document.</li>
    <li>Confirm the permitted length of stay for your visit.</li>
    <li>Prepare any required visa, entry permit, endorsement, or supporting documents in advance.</li>
    <li>Recheck the official information shortly before departure.</li>
  </ul>
